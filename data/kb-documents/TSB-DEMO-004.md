## Affected Vehicles

- Orion Dynamics Cruiser V6 (2020–2023)
- Orion Dynamics Cruiser V6 Touring (2021–2023)

## Symptom Description

Driver reports a noticeable vibration between 1800–2200 RPM during light throttle acceleration. The vibration is accompanied by a low-frequency resonance audible inside the cabin. Condition is most noticeable in 3rd and 4th gear under partial load. No check engine light or diagnostic codes are stored.

## Root Cause

The dual-mass flywheel spring dampener assemblies experience premature wear at the secondary mass pivot points due to insufficient lubrication retention in the spring channel. This allows torsional oscillations from the engine to pass through to the transmission input shaft undampened within the affected RPM range.

## Corrective Action

1. Remove transmission and inspect dual-mass flywheel for excessive free play (specification: max 12 mm arc travel at outer ring).
2. Replace dual-mass flywheel with revised assembly incorporating improved spring channel lubrication retention features.
3. Replace transmission input shaft pilot bearing as a precautionary measure.
4. Refill transmission fluid to specification and perform adaptive shift relearn procedure.
