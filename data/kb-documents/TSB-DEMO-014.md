## Affected Vehicles

- Crestline Automotive Traverse (2022–2024)
- Crestline Automotive Traverse Sport (2023–2024)

## Symptom Description

Owners report a single loud "clunk" noise from the front suspension when transitioning from braking to acceleration at low speeds (10–30 km/h), such as during stop-and-go traffic. The noise is most noticeable on uneven road surfaces. Some owners also report a slight steering wheel vibration (shimmy) at highway speeds between 100–120 km/h that was not present when the vehicle was new. No warning lights or diagnostic codes are associated with these symptoms.

## Root Cause

The front stabilizer bar end link ball joints use a nylon liner that undergoes cold-flow deformation under sustained load. After 15,000–30,000 km of driving, the liner develops sufficient clearance to allow the ball stud to shift position during load reversal (braking-to-acceleration transition), producing the reported clunk. The progressive wear also introduces play in the stabilizer bar geometry, which manifests as a steering shimmy at highway speeds due to asymmetric bar preload.

## Corrective Action

1. Inspect front stabilizer bar end links for play by attempting to move the ball stud with the stabilizer bar loaded. More than 1 mm of axial movement indicates replacement is required.
2. Replace both front stabilizer bar end links with revised design using glass-filled PTFE liner (improved cold-flow resistance).
3. Inspect stabilizer bar bushings (frame-mounted) for cracking or deformation. Replace if bushing deflection exceeds 2 mm under 50 Nm test load.
4. Torque all end link fasteners to specification (85 Nm ±5 Nm) with thread-locking compound.
5. Perform a road test at low speed (stop-and-go) and highway speed (100–120 km/h) to confirm noise and vibration are resolved.
